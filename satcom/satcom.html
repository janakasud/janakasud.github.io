Satellite communication is a vital technology that uses orbiting satellites to relay radio signals between Earth stations. It enables global coverage for telecommunications, broadcasting, navigation, and remote sensing.

Key Components of a SatCom System

  • **The Space Segment:** The satellite itself (transponder, antenna, power source, etc.) orbiting the Earth.
  • **The Ground Segment (Earth Stations):** Antennas and equipment used to transmit to and receive from the satellite.
  • **The Control Segment:** Facilities that track and monitor the satellite's health and orbital position.

Types of Satellite Orbits

Abbreviation Orbit Name Approximate Altitude
**GEO** Geostationary Earth Orbit $35,786 \text{ km}$
**MEO** Medium Earth Orbit $2,000 \text{ km} \text{ to } 35,786 \text{ km}$
**LEO** Low Earth Orbit $160 \text{ km} \text{ to } 2,000 \text{ km}$
**Did you know?** GEO satellites appear stationary from the ground, making them ideal for broadcasting and fixed-point communication.
