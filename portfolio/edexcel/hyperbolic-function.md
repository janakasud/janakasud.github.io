## Recap: Trigonometric Functions
- Unit circle points: $(\cos \theta, \sin \theta)$  
- Identity: $\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1$  
- Ratios: $\tan \theta = \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}$  
- Parametric form: $x(\theta) = \cos \theta,\; y(\theta) = \sin \theta$  

## Hyperbolic Counterparts
- Unit hyperbola: $x^2 - y^2 = 1$  
- Parameterisation: $(\cosh t, \sinh t)$  
- Identity: $\cosh^2 t - \sinh^2 t = 1$  
- Ratio: $\tanh t = \dfrac{\sinh t}{\cosh t}$  
- Symmetry mirrors the asymptotes $y = \pm x$.

## Why They Matter
- Model growth/decay, catenaries, rapidity (relativity), and show up in calculus/differential equations.
- Algebra parallels familiar trig identities.

## Quick Reference
### Core definitions
- $\sinh t = \dfrac{e^{t} - e^{-t}}{2}$
- $\cosh t = \dfrac{e^{t} + e^{-t}}{2}$
- $\tanh t = \dfrac{\sinh t}{\cosh t}$

### Reciprocal forms
- $\coth t = \dfrac{\cosh t}{\sinh t}$
- $\operatorname{sech} t = \dfrac{1}{\cosh t}$
- $\operatorname{cosech} t = \dfrac{1}{\sinh t}$
